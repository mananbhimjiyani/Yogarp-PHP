Types of Yoga

Nisha's Yoga Studio offers a diverse range of yoga styles to cater to various preferences and needs. Here are some of the types of yoga you can experience at Nisha's Yoga Studio:

Authentic Yoga

This classic form of yoga focuses on physical postures, breathing techniques, and relaxation. It provides a solid foundation for beginners and helps improve flexibility and balance.

Aerial Yoga, Wall Bar Yoga, Rope Yoga

involves performing yoga postures while suspended in silk hammocks, allowing for greater flexibility, strength, and decompression of the spine.

Astang Yoga

is a structured and physically demanding practice that follows a specific sequence of postures and synchronized breathing. It is known for its progressive nature and is ideal for those seeking a disciplined practice.

Vinyasa Yoga

is a dynamic, flowing style of yoga where poses are synchronized with breath. It builds strength, endurance, and promotes mindfulness through continuous movement.

Prop Yoga

emphasizes precision and alignment in poses. It uses props like belts and blocks to aid in achieving proper alignment, making it accessible for practitioners of all levels. It is also known as Iyengar Yoga.

Power Yoga

A vigorous and fitness-oriented yoga style, Power Yoga combines strength-building exercises with yoga poses. It helps improve cardiovascular health, flexibility, and muscle tone.

Kundalini Yoga

focuses on awakening the dormant energy at the base of the spine through a combination of postures, breathwork, and meditation. It aims to promote spiritual growth and self-awareness.

Yin Yoga

involves holding passive poses for extended periods, targeting the deep connective tissues and promoting relaxation and flexibility.

Restorative Yoga

This gentle practice uses props to support the body in relaxing poses, encouraging deep relaxation, stress relief, and restoration.

Who can take your yoga classes?

All are welcome to join our yoga classes, regardless of age, prior experience, or fitness level. We offer classes tailored to beginners, intermediate practitioners, and advanced practitioners.

Do I need prior yoga experience to join your classes?

No prior experience is necessary. Our classes are designed to accommodate individuals of all skill levels, and our instructors provide modifications and adjustments as needed.

How do I sign up for a class?

You can sign up for a class online, by phone, or in person at the studio.

I have a health condition. Can I still take yoga classes?

Yes, yoga can be beneficial for people with a variety of health conditions. However, it is important to talk to your doctor before starting any new exercise program, including yoga.

What should I wear to yoga class?

We recommend wearing comfortable, breathable clothing that allows you to move freely..

What should I eat before and during classes?

To optimize your yoga practice, it's advisable to ensure your stomach is empty during your session. This means having a full meal at least 3.5 hours before, enjoying breakfast or supper a minimum of 2 hours before, and consuming water or liquids about 30 minutes prior to your class.

Do I need to bring my own yoga mat?

Certainly, you are required to bring your own yoga mat. If you register, you can store your yoga mat with your identification mark in the cupboard in the classroom.

Do you offer private yoga lessons?

Yes, we offer private yoga lessons upon request. Please contact us to learn more.

Yes, we believe in fostering a sense of community. We regularly host social gatherings, workshops, and events where you can connect with other like-minded individuals who share your interest in yoga and holistic wellness. Check our website or social media for updates on upcoming events.

What are the benefits of yoga?

Yoga has many benefits for both the body and mind. Some of the benefits of yoga include:

  • Increased flexibility and strength
  • Improved balance and coordination
  • Reduced stress and anxiety
  • Improved sleep quality
  • Increased self-awareness
  • Reduced pain and inflammation
  • Improved cardiovascular health
  • Reduced risk of injury
